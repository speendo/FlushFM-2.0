# US-0007: Clean Audio Stop Before Power-Off

> **Status:** To Do  
> **Priority:** High  
> **Created:** 2026-03-11

---

## User Story

As a **user**,  
I want **audio to stop immediately and cleanly when the system decides to cut power (relay off / sleep)**,  
so that **music does not continue playing for several seconds after the light sensor triggers a shutdown**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] A `stopAndWait()` method is added to `IAudioPlayer` that stops the stream and blocks until the I2S DMA buffer is drained (silence on output)
- [ ] The relay-off / sleep sequence always calls `stopAndWait()` before opening the relay or entering sleep
- [ ] Audio stops within ≤ 500 ms of a stop request under normal conditions
- [ ] After `stopAndWait()` returns, no audible sound is produced even if the relay is opened immediately
- [ ] Calling `stopAndWait()` when no stream is active is a no-op (safe to call unconditionally)

---

## Notes

**Root cause:** `audio.stopSong()` (ESP32-audioI2S) stops the decoder and HTTP client immediately, but the I2S DMA peripheral continues draining its already-filled hardware buffers independently of the CPU. The drain takes several seconds at 256 kbps with a large PSRAM input buffer.

**Approach options:**
1. Call `audio.stopSong()` then poll `audio.isRunning()` with a timeout until false
2. ~~Reduce DMA buffer size to minimise drain time~~ – **rejected**: unacceptable impact on dropout resilience at high bitrates
3. Use the I2S peripheral's `i2s_zero_dma_buffer()` IDF call to flush immediately
4. Set volume to 0 via `setVolume(0)` before stopping – takes effect immediately in the I2S output stage, producing silence while the DMA drains in the background; simple and stays within the `IAudioPlayer` abstraction

Option 4 is the preferred first approach: instant silence, no IDF dependency, no polling. Can be combined with option 1 to also free the HTTP connection and decoder resources. Option 3 remains as fallback if a hard DMA flush is ever needed.

**Observed behavior (US-0003):** `vTaskSuspend(audioTask)` causes audio to keep playing for several seconds – do not use suspend as a stop mechanism in production.
